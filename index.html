What are cryptocurrencies?

Cryptocurrencies are digital assets that use cryptography to secure transactions and control the creation of new units. They work on blockchain technology, which is a decentralized and distributed ledger maintained by a network of computers.

How Cryptocurrencies Work

User Creates Transaction
↓
Signed with Private Key
↓
Broadcast to Network
↓
Transaction Verified
↓
Consensus (PoW / PoS)
↓
Block Added to Blockchain
↓
Transaction Confirmed

Popular cryptocurrencies

Bitcoin (BTC)

Bitcoin is the first and most valuable cryptocurrency. It is often called digital gold and is used as a store of value.

Ethereum (ETH)

Ethereum powers smart contracts and decentralized applications. It is the foundation of DeFi and NFTs.

Binance Coin (BNB)

BNB is used for trading fee discounts and applications on the Binance Smart Chain ecosystem.

Solana (SOL)

Solana is known for high-speed transactions and low fees, making it popular for scalable blockchain applications.

Ripple (XRP)

XRP focuses on fast and low-cost international payments, mainly used by financial institutions.

Cardano (ADA)

Cardano is a research-driven blockchain platform designed for security, sustainability, and scalability.

Dogecoin (DOGE)

Dogecoin started as a meme but became popular for fast transactions and strong community support.

Polkadot (DOT)

Polkadot enables different blockchains to connect and share information securely.
